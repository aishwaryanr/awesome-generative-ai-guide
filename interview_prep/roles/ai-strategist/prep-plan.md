# Prep Plan

Two paths: a 3-week plan if you have runway, and a 1-week crunch if you do not. Both sequence the rounds, questions, resources, and courses into a day-by-day path. Adjust the weighting to your buyer type (consultancy leans case, enterprise leans stakeholder and governance, AI-forward leans technical decomposition), using [README.md](README.md).

The single highest-leverage habit for this role: do timed, out-loud reps of the case and the stakeholder round. Reading is necessary; speaking under a clock is what gets you hired.

## The 3-week plan

### Week 1: Build fluency (so no technical round can rattle you)

**Day 1: Map the role and the loop.**
Read [README.md](README.md) and [rounds.md](rounds.md) end to end. Read the repo topic pages [Foundations](../../../topics/foundations.md) and [Prompting and context](../../../topics/prompting.md). Watch [Karpathy: Intro to LLMs](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zjkBMFhNj_g). Write your 90-second background narrative ending on outcomes.

**Day 2: RAG and knowledge.**
Read [RAG topic](../../../topics/rag.md) and [Agentic RAG 101](../../../resources/agentic_rag_101.md). Answer questions 9 to 13 in [questions.md](questions.md) out loud without looking. Skim the [RAG research table](../../../research_updates/rag_research_table.md).

**Day 3: Fine-tuning and the decision ladder.**
Read [Fine-tuning topic](../../../topics/fine-tuning.md) and [Fine-tuning 101](../../../resources/fine_tuning_101.md). Nail the prompt-then-RAG-then-fine-tune-then-agent decision order (question 13) cold, because it recurs across rounds.

**Day 4: Agents, MCP, context, reasoning.**
Read [Agents topic](../../../topics/agents.md) and [Anthropic: Building effective agents](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/building-effective-agents). Skim [MCP introduction](https://modelcontextprotocol.io/introduction) and [Anthropic: context engineering](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/effective-context-engineering-for-ai-agents). Answer questions 14 to 21 out loud. Start the [Agentic AI Crash Course](../../../free_courses/agentic_ai_crash_course/README.md).

**Day 5: Evaluation.**
Read [Evaluation topic](../../../topics/evaluation.md) and begin [AI Evals for Everyone](../../../free_courses/ai_evals_for_everyone/README.md). Answer questions 22 to 26. Be able to explain why evals are non-negotiable in two sentences.

**Day 6: Cost, latency, production.**
Read [Production topic](../../../topics/production.md). Answer questions 27 to 30. Practice explaining total cost of ownership and why pilots die between pilot and production.

**Day 7: Consolidate and self-test.**
Do a closed-book pass of all Section A to E questions (1 to 30). Mark the ones you fumbled and reread just those topics. Skim the repo's [60 GenAI interview questions](../../60_gen_ai_questions.md) for anything new.

### Week 2: Build strategy judgment (the case and the numbers)

**Day 8: The market reality.**
Read the [State of AI 2025 report](../../../research_updates/state_of_ai_2025_report/README.md) and the [Stanford HAI 2025 AI Index](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2025-ai-index-report). Memorize 5 or 6 citable numbers (adoption, pilot-failure rate, ROI concentration). Read [Deloitte: State of Gen AI in the Enterprise](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/what-we-do/capabilities/applied-artificial-intelligence/content/state-of-generative-ai-in-enterprise.html).

**Day 9: Prioritization and ROI.**
Study questions 31, 32, 36, 37. Build one full ROI model on paper for a made-up use case: baseline, lift, full cost, payback. Read the [GenAI roadmap](../../../resources/genai_roadmap.md).

**Day 10: Build vs buy vs partner.**
Study questions 33, 34, 35, 38. Read [a16z: enterprise AI 2025](https://a16z.com/ai-enterprise-2025/). Write your own one-paragraph build-vs-buy heuristic you can recite.

**Day 11: First full case, timed.**
Take one case prompt from [rounds.md](rounds.md) section 3. Give yourself 40 minutes, out loud, recorded. Use the 8-step structure. Then critique your own recording against the "what good looks like" list.

**Day 12: Second and third cases.**
Run two more case prompts, 40 minutes each. Rotate industries (bank, hospital, SaaS, retailer). Focus on the two weakest steps from yesterday, usually prioritization and quantification.

**Day 13: Technical decomposition.**
Read section 4 of [rounds.md](rounds.md). Take one decomposition prompt and practice the walking-skeleton MVP approach out loud. Cover data, auth, evals, failure modes, rollback. Skip lightly if your loop is a pure consultancy case.

**Day 14: Governance and risk.**
Read [Safety and security topic](../../../topics/safety-security.md) and [Securing agentic AI systems](../../../resources/securing_agentic_ai_systems.md). Learn the [NIST AI RMF](https://airc.nist.gov/AI_RMF_Knowledge_Base/AI_RMF) four functions and skim the [EU AI Act explorer](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/). Answer questions 39 to 42.

### Week 3: Communication, behavioral, and polish

**Day 15: Stakeholder simulation.**
Read section 5 of [rounds.md](rounds.md). Practice 3 scenarios out loud: convince a skeptical CFO, deliver bad news about accuracy, talk a CEO down from a 90-day agent. Record and check for jargon, defensiveness, and ownership language.

**Day 16: Change management and adoption.**
Study questions 43 and 44. Read [Ethan Mollick: One Useful Thing](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/) for grounded adoption framing. Prepare a real story of driving adoption without authority.

**Day 17: Behavioral bank.**
Write out 6 STAR stories: a failed or stalled AI initiative, driving adoption, resetting expectations when tech could not deliver, a time you said no, a high-stakes call under uncertainty, and cross-functional influence. Put a number in each.

**Day 18: Full mock loop, part 1.**
Simulate the fluency round (rapid-fire questions 1 to 30) plus one timed case. Ideally with a friend playing interviewer who interrupts.

**Day 19: Full mock loop, part 2.**
Simulate the stakeholder round and the behavioral round back to back. Then take one take-home prompt and produce a 6-slide outline in 90 minutes.

**Day 20: Weak-spot repair.**
Reread only the topics and questions you keep fumbling. Refresh your citable numbers. Prepare your own questions to ask (section 8 of [rounds.md](rounds.md)).

**Day 21: Light review and rest.**
Reread [README.md](README.md), your 90-second narrative, your build-vs-buy heuristic, and your 6 numbers. Do not cram new material. Rest.

## The 1-week crunch plan

For when the loop is in 7 days. Triage hard: fluency, one case rep per day, and the stakeholder round.

**Day 1: Orientation and fluency core.**
Read [README.md](README.md) and [rounds.md](rounds.md). Watch [Karpathy: Intro to LLMs](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zjkBMFhNj_g). Answer questions 1 to 13 out loud. Lock the prompt-RAG-fine-tune-agent decision ladder.

**Day 2: Agents, evals, cost.**
Read [Anthropic: Building effective agents](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/building-effective-agents). Answer questions 14 to 30. Be able to explain agents, evals, and total cost of ownership cold.

**Day 3: Strategy and numbers.**
Read the [State of AI 2025 report](../../../research_updates/state_of_ai_2025_report/README.md) and memorize 5 citable numbers. Study questions 31 to 38. Build one ROI model on paper. Write your build-vs-buy heuristic.

**Day 4: First timed case.**
Run one case from [rounds.md](rounds.md), 40 minutes, out loud, recorded. Self-critique against the good-looks-like list.

**Day 5: Governance plus second case.**
Learn the [NIST AI RMF](https://airc.nist.gov/AI_RMF_Knowledge_Base/AI_RMF) four functions and skim the [EU AI Act explorer](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/). Answer questions 39 to 42. Run a second timed case in a different industry.

**Day 6: Communication and behavioral.**
Practice 3 stakeholder scenarios out loud (questions and section 5). Write 5 STAR stories with numbers, including a failure and a time you said no.

**Day 7: Mock and rest.**
Do one compressed mock: 15 minutes rapid-fire fluency, one 30-minute case, one stakeholder scenario. Refresh your narrative, heuristic, and numbers. Rest.

## Readiness checklist

You are ready when you can, without notes:

- Give the prompt-RAG-fine-tune-agent decision ladder and defend each step.
- Explain when an agent is worth its cost and name its failure modes.
- State why evals are non-negotiable and how you would evaluate a RAG system.
- Prioritize use cases with an explicit 5-factor rubric and build an ROI model with a baseline.
- Make a build-vs-buy-vs-partner call and justify it.
- Name the NIST AI RMF functions and speak credibly about the EU AI Act's risk tiers.
- Explain why most pilots die and what you do about it.
- Cite 5 or 6 current market numbers.
- Run a 40-minute case out loud with a clear recommendation.
- Handle a skeptical stakeholder without jargon or defensiveness.
- Tell 5 STAR stories, each with a number and a lesson.
