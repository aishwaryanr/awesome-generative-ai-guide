# Courses

The best free courses for an AI Strategist, starting with this repository's own (built for exactly this fluency) and then verified external ones. Every external link returned HTTP 200 at the time of writing. You do not need to finish all of these. Pick based on your weakest round in [rounds.md](rounds.md).

---

## From this repository (do these first)

- [Agentic AI Crash Course](../../../free_courses/agentic_ai_crash_course/README.md): the fastest way to build correct intuition about agents, tools, memory, and when they are worth it. Directly prepares you for the agents questions in the fluency round.
- [AI Evals for Everyone](../../../free_courses/ai_evals_for_everyone/README.md): builds the evaluation fluency that is non-negotiable for this role. If you can only do one repo course before the fluency round, do this one, because "how do you know it works" is asked in every loop.
- [Generative AI Genius](../../../free_courses/generative_ai_genius/README.md): a broad, plain-language grounding in generative AI for those coming from a business background. Builds the vocabulary you need to translate for stakeholders.
- [Applied LLMs Mastery 2024](../../../free_courses/Applied_LLMs_Mastery_2024/README.md): a structured walk through the applied LLM stack (prompting, RAG, fine-tuning, evaluation, deployment). Builds the end-to-end picture a strategist must hold to pressure-test a technical plan.
- The full [courses index](../../../courses.md) in this repo, grouped by topic, if you want to go deeper on any single area (RAG, fine-tuning, agents, production).

## AI fundamentals and fluency (external, free)

- [Google Cloud: What is generative AI](https://cloud.google.com/discover/what-is-generative-ai) and [Google machine learning and AI training](https://cloud.google.com/learn/training/machinelearning-ai): free, structured foundations from a major cloud provider, useful for the vendor-neutral basics.
- [Kaggle: 5-Day Gen AI Intensive](https://www.kaggle.com/learn-guide/5-day-genai): a free, self-paced intensive by Google covering foundation models, prompting, embeddings, agents, and MLOps for generative AI. Builds a solid week-one base across the whole stack.
- [Elements of AI](https://www.elementsofai.com/): a free, non-technical university course on what AI is and is not. Good for filling conceptual gaps and for framing AI in plain language.
- [Microsoft: Generative AI for Beginners (GitHub course)](https://github.com/microsoft/generative-ai-for-beginners) and the [companion video series](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/shows/generative-ai-for-beginners/): 18+ lessons from fundamentals through building applications. Builds hands-on understanding of what your engineers do.
- [Microsoft: AI for Beginners](https://github.com/microsoft/AI-For-Beginners) ([web version](https://microsoft.github.io/AI-For-Beginners/)): a broader 12-week curriculum on AI concepts, useful if you want the machine-learning context beneath generative AI.

## Agents, tools, and the current stack (external, free)

- [Hugging Face: AI Agents Course](https://huggingface.co/learn/agents-course/unit0/introduction): a free, hands-on course on agent design, tools, and evaluation. Builds real intuition for the technical decomposition round.
- [Hugging Face: all free courses](https://huggingface.co/learn): the hub, including NLP and LLM courses if you want deeper technical grounding.
- [Anthropic: Building effective agents](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/building-effective-agents) and [OpenAI: A practical guide to building agents (PDF)](https://cdn.openai.com/business-guides-and-resources/a-practical-guide-to-building-agents.pdf): not full courses, but the two best short reads to pair with any agents course. Build the judgment for when an agent is worth it.

## Prompting and context (external, free)

- [Anthropic: prompt engineering interactive tutorial](https://github.com/anthropics/prompt-eng-interactive-tutorial): a free, hands-on tutorial that builds practical prompting skill. Cheap, high-leverage fluency.
- [Prompting Guide](https://www.promptingguide.ai/): a comprehensive free reference covering prompting and context techniques, updated for current models.
- [Anthropic courses hub](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/): free official courses on the API, prompting, and building with models.

## Applied learning platform (external, free tier)

- [DeepLearning.AI short courses](https://www.deeplearning.ai/short-courses/) and the [full course catalog](https://www.deeplearning.ai/courses/): a large library of free, short, project-based courses on RAG, agents, evaluation, and building LLM applications. Pick the ones matching your weakest topic. Note: skip any evals-specific course fronted by external instructors and use this repo's [AI Evals for Everyone](../../../free_courses/ai_evals_for_everyone/README.md) instead.
- [learn.deeplearning.ai](https://learn.deeplearning.ai/): the platform hub where the courses are hosted.

## Governance and responsible AI (external, free)

- [NIST AI RMF Knowledge Base](https://airc.nist.gov/AI_RMF_Knowledge_Base/AI_RMF): a navigable, playbook-style walkthrough of the risk-management framework you will reference in the case and governance questions. Read the four functions and the playbook.
- [EU AI Act explorer](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/): a structured, readable walkthrough of the risk tiers and obligations. Enough to speak credibly about regulation in a strategy.

## How to sequence courses against the loop

- Weak on **AI fluency**: Generative AI Genius, then Kaggle 5-Day Gen AI, then Anthropic's prompting tutorial.
- Weak on **agents / technical decomposition**: Agentic AI Crash Course, then Hugging Face Agents Course, then the two agent guides.
- Weak on **evals / quality**: AI Evals for Everyone (do it fully).
- Weak on **governance**: the NIST Knowledge Base and the EU AI Act explorer.
- Weak on **business framing**: read the market and ROI resources in [resources.md](resources.md); courses matter less here than reps on cases.
