# :star2: Most Impactful AI Evaluation Papers 
### (January 2025 to June 2026)

The field of AI evaluation has experienced growth in 2025, driven by the need for robust, comprehensive, and reliable assessment methods for AI systems. As Large Language Models (LLMs) and Multimodal AI systems become more capable and widely deployed, the development of effective evaluation frameworks has become important for ensuring their quality, safety, and reliability. This table provides summaries of papers published in 2025, covering various aspects of AI evaluation research. These topics include:

1. **LLM Judges & Automated Evaluation**: Methods for using AI systems to evaluate other AI systems, including LLM-as-a-judge approaches.
2. **Benchmarks & Datasets**: Comprehensive evaluation benchmarks and datasets for assessing AI model performance.
3. **Domain-Specific Evaluation**: Specialized evaluation frameworks tailored for specific domains like finance, science, and medicine.
4. **Multimodal Evaluation**: Assessment methods for vision-language, audio-visual, and other multimodal AI systems.
5. **Reasoning & Cognitive Evaluation**: Frameworks for evaluating reasoning, logic, and cognitive capabilities in AI models.
6. **Evaluation Methodologies**: Novel approaches and frameworks for AI system assessment.
7. **Robustness & Safety Evaluation**: Methods for assessing model robustness, safety, and reliability.
8. **Evaluation Surveys**: Comprehensive reviews and surveys of evaluation approaches and methodologies.

This table will continue to be updated regularly as new evaluation research emerges!

| [Video-Based Reward Modeling for Computer-Use Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10178) | This paper studies reward modeling from execution video as a scalable way to evaluate whether a computer-using agent's trajectory fulfills a user instruction, independent of the agent's internal reasoning or actions. The authors build ExeVR-53k, a dataset of 53k video-task-reward triplets, use adversarial instruction translation to synthesize step-annotated negatives, and apply spatiotemporal token pruning to handle long, high-resolution execution videos. The resulting ExeVRM 8B model, which takes only an instruction and a video-execution sequence, achieves 84.7% accuracy and 87.7% recall and outperforms proprietary models including GPT-5.2 and Gemini-3 Pro across Ubuntu, macOS, Windows, and Android. Because it depends only on observed execution rather than agent internals, the approach offers a model-agnostic evaluator that generalizes across operating systems and agent implementations. | LLM Judges & Automated Evaluation | March 2026 |
| [RubricBench: Aligning Model-Generated Rubrics with Human Standards](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01562) | As reward models shift toward rubric-guided evaluation to reduce surface-level bias, this paper introduces RubricBench to test the reliability of that paradigm. The benchmark contains 1,147 pairwise comparisons built through a multi-dimensional filtration pipeline that targets hard samples with nuanced complexity and misleading surface cues, each augmented with expert-annotated atomic rubrics derived strictly from instructions. Experiments reveal a substantial capability gap between human-annotated and model-generated rubrics, showing that even state-of-the-art models struggle to autonomously specify valid evaluation criteria and lag considerably behind human-guided performance. The finding generalizes to any evaluation setup that relies on models to write their own grading rubrics. | Evaluation Methodologies | March 2026 |
| [SWE-rebench V2: Language-Agnostic SWE Task Collection at Scale](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23866) | This paper presents a language-agnostic automated pipeline for harvesting executable real-world software engineering tasks and constructing reproducible training and evaluation environments. The pipeline synthesizes repository-specific installation and test procedures via an interactive setup agent and filters unsound instances using an ensemble of LLM judges validated against human-verified SWE-bench annotations. It yields a dataset of over 32,000 tasks spanning 20 languages and more than 3,600 repositories with pre-built images, plus an additional 120,000-plus tasks with fail-to-pass tests and metadata. A diagnostic study across five programming languages and seven models flags common confounders such as overly restrictive tests and underspecified descriptions, offering a scalable template for building reliable multi-language coding benchmarks. | Benchmarks & Datasets | February 2026 |
| [AgentVista: Evaluating Multimodal Agents in Ultra-Challenging Realistic Visual Scenarios](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23166) | AgentVista is a benchmark for generalist multimodal agents that must solve multi-step workflows grounded in visual evidence, addressing the gap that existing multimodal benchmarks mainly test single-turn reasoning or narrow tool skills. It spans 25 sub-domains across 7 categories, pairing detail-rich visual scenarios with hybrid tool use including web search, image search, page navigation, and code-based operations. The design generalizes toward measuring realistic, long-horizon agent behavior rather than isolated visual questions. | Multimodal Evaluation | February 2026 |
| [SQuTR: A Robustness Benchmark for Spoken Query to Text Retrieval under Acoustic Noise](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12783) | SQuTR is a robustness benchmark for spoken query to text retrieval that addresses the limitation of existing datasets to simple queries under constrained noise. It aggregates 37,317 unique queries from six English and Chinese retrieval datasets, synthesizes speech using voice profiles from 200 real speakers, and mixes 17 categories of real-world environmental noise under controlled signal-to-noise ratios for reproducible evaluation. Under a unified protocol, large-scale tests of cascaded and end-to-end systems show retrieval performance degrading as noise rises, with substantially different drops across systems and even large models struggling under extreme noise. The controlled-perturbation methodology provides a reproducible diagnostic testbed that generalizes to robustness studies of retrieval under real acoustic conditions. | Robustness & Safety Evaluation | February 2026 |
| [GENIUS: Generative Fluid Intelligence Evaluation Suite](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11144) | GENIUS targets a blind spot in unified multimodal model evaluation: existing benchmarks measure crystallized intelligence that relies on recalled knowledge rather than generative fluid intelligence, the capacity to induce patterns, reason through constraints, and adapt on the fly.
